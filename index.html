What is Brisket?

Brisket comes from the chest of a animal. As consumers, we usually get our Brisket meat from cows. Beef Brisket is a working muscle of the cow, giving it a tough mouthfeel when cooked the wrong way.

How do we cook Brisket

There are many ways you can cook a brisket such as in the oven or in the smoker. The most commonly known way to cook Brisket is with a smoker.

Cooking Machines

Any time of smoker will work. Common ones are electric, pellets, and offset. Find what works best for you.

How to Pick Brisket at the Store

Picking a Brisket is one of the most important steps to bbq. Small details such as shape, bend test, and grades add up. Paying attention to the small details in everything set you apart from your competition.

The Grades of Brisket

There are many other grades of brisket, but for simplicity, I will only talk about the commonly used grades often found at your nearest supermarket.

  • Prime
  • Choice
  • Select
  • Unmarked

Visible Fat

Purchasing a Brisket that contains too much fat produces less of that meat you are looking for. A fatty Brisket will require a large trim in order to cook properly on the smoker. A good tip for limiting the fat would be the bend test. If you can bend your Brisket from one end to another, your Brisket passes the bend test.

The Shape

What we are looking for is a oval shape, that is the same thickness on both ends. Brisket is often uneven, having a thinner flat which can dry out the meat when cooking.
